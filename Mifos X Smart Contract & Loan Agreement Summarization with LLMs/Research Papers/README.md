## RAG for Legal & Financial Contracts

### LegalBench-RAG: A Benchmark for Retrieval-Augmented Generation in the Legal Domain
**Authors:** Nicholas Pipitone, Ghita Houir Alami  
**Venue:** arXiv 2024  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.10343  
**Code:** https://github.com/zeroentropy-ai/legalbenchrag

**Summary:**  
The first benchmark specifically designed to evaluate the *retrieval step* of RAG pipelines in the legal domain — filling a gap left by LegalBench, which only tests generative capabilities. Built by retracing LegalBench query contexts back to their source documents, resulting in 6,858 expert-annotated query-answer pairs over a corpus of 79M+ characters covering NDAs, M&A agreements, commercial contracts, and privacy policies.

**Key Findings:**
- Precise snippet-level retrieval is critical; retrieving large imprecise chunks inflates cost, latency, and hallucination rates.
- Recursive Character Text Splitter (RCTS) chunking significantly outperforms naive fixed-size chunking.
- Generic rerankers (e.g., Cohere Reranker) *hurt* performance on legal text, suggesting the need for domain-specific rerankers.
- Privacy policy queries are easier for AI than complex M&A or financial documents with dense jargon.

### Towards Reliable Retrieval in RAG Systems for Large Legal Datasets (Summary-Augmented Chunking)
**Authors:** Reuter et al.  
**Venue:** Natural Legal Language Processing Workshop 2025 (NLLP @ ACL)  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.06999

**Summary:**  
Identifies and quantifies **Document-Level Retrieval Mismatch (DRM)** — a failure mode where a RAG pipeline retrieves a chunk from the *wrong* source document entirely. Proposes **Summary-Augmented Chunking (SAC)**, which prepends a short LLM-generated summary of each document to every chunk, injecting global context to help the retriever disambiguate between similar documents.

**Key Findings:**
- DRM is a major but underdiagnosed failure mode in standard RAG pipelines for legal corpora.
- SAC substantially reduces DRM across all datasets in LegalBench-RAG.
- Surprisingly, **generic summarization outperforms expert-guided summarization** for retrieval performance — domain-specific prompting does not always help.
- The method is lightweight and modular, compatible with any existing RAG pipeline.

### LRAGE: Legal Retrieval Augmented Generation Evaluation Tool
**Authors:** (Anonymous / EMNLP 2025 submission)  
**Venue:** arXiv 2025  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.01840

**Summary:**  
An open-source evaluation tool with a GUI that allows systematic benchmarking of RAG systems across multiple legal benchmarks (LegalBench, KBL, LawBench). Provides pre-compiled BM25 and FAISS indexes and preprocessed legal corpora (Pile-of-Law) for immediate experimentation. Evaluates the impact of retrieval corpus, retriever type, backbone LLM, reranker, and evaluation rubric.

**Key Findings:**
- Even the best commercial RAG system exhibits a **~17% hallucination rate** on U.S. legal tasks.
- BM25 and current dense retrievers show limited performance on legal-domain passage retrieval.
- Dense retrievers underperform BM25 in the legal domain, suggesting domain adaptation of dense retrievers is critical.
- Using a domain-specific legal corpus dramatically outperforms Wikipedia as a retrieval source.

### Enhancing the Precision and Interpretability of RAG in Legal Technology: A Survey
**Authors:** Hindi et al.  
**Venue:** IEEE Access 2025  
**DOI:** 10.1109/ACCESS.2025.3550145

**Summary:**  
A comprehensive survey of RAG methods applied to legal technology, covering retrieval sources, embedding models, augmentation mechanisms, and downstream applications. Maps the landscape of legal RAG systems across privacy law, legislative texts, criminal law, statutory law, and immigration law.

**Key Findings:**
- Dense retrievers generally outperform sparse retrievers (BM25) in capturing semantic similarity for legal queries.
- RAG applications in law surged in 2024, with use cases spanning QA, legal advice, chatbots, and judgment prediction.
- Three dedicated legal RAG datasets were introduced in 2024 for benchmarking.
- Significant open challenges remain in multi-lingual legal RAG and low-resource jurisdictions.

## Multi-Agent Orchestration for Financial Documents

### Benchmarking Multi-Agent LLM Architectures for Financial Document Processing
**Authors:** (Anonymous)  
**Venue:** arXiv 2025  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.22651

**Summary:**  
The most comprehensive empirical benchmark comparing multi-agent orchestration patterns for financial document processing. Evaluates four architectures — **sequential pipeline**, **parallel fan-out with merge**, **hierarchical supervisor-worker**, and **reflexive self-correcting loop** — across 5 frontier LLMs on 10,000 SEC filings (10-K, 10-Q, 8-K) spanning 25 extraction field types including governance structures, executive compensation, and financial metrics.

**Key Findings:**
- **Reflexive (self-correcting) architecture** achieves the highest field-level F1 (0.943) but costs 2.3× more than sequential.
- **Hierarchical supervisor-worker** offers the best cost-accuracy tradeoff for production deployment.
- Sequential pipelines are cheapest but least accurate; parallel fan-out improves throughput without strong accuracy gains.
- Temperature should be set to 0.0 for extraction agents and ~0.3 for supervisor/critique agents.
- Results may not generalize to non-English (e.g., IFRS) filings.

### LAW: Legal Agentic Workflows for Custody and Fund Services Contracts
**Authors:** William Watson, Nicole Cho, Nishan Srishankar, Zhen Zeng, Lucas Cecchi, Daniel Scott, Suchetha Siddagangappa, Rachneet Kaur, Tucker Balch, Manuela Veloso  
**Venue:** COLING 2025 Industry Track  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.11063

**Summary:**  
Introduces LAW, a modular agentic system targeting financial legal contracts in the custody and fund services domain (e.g., fee schedules, indemnification rights, termination clauses). Rather than fine-tuning a legal LLM, LAW orchestrates a suite of reusable domain-specific tools and text agents on top of general-purpose LLMs, significantly reducing cost and improving flexibility.

**Key Findings:**
- Off-the-shelf LLMs struggle with financial contracts due to long unstructured text, limited context windows, and dense legal jargon.
- LAW surpasses the naive LLM baseline by **+92.9 percentage points** on complex tasks like calculating termination dates.
- Reusable domain-specific tools are a **cost-effective alternative to fine-tuning** dedicated legal LLMs.
- Agentic tool orchestration supports both closed-source (GPT-4) and open-source LLMs without retraining.

## Combined RAG + Agentic Systems

### Dynamic Multi-Agent Orchestration and Retrieval for Multi-Source Question-Answer Systems
**Authors:** (Anonymous)  
**Venue:** arXiv 2024  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.17964

**Summary:**  
Proposes a multi-source QA framework that combines RAG, Text-to-SQL, dynamic prompt engineering, and agent-based orchestration. A **RAG Agent** handles interpretive questions over unstructured legal text via vector store retrieval, while a **SQL Agent** handles precise structured queries — both coordinated by an orchestrator that routes queries to the best agent based on the nature of the question.

**Key Findings:**
- Combining RAG and Text-to-SQL agents outperforms single-modality approaches on multi-source queries.
- Agent-based routing is especially effective for contract management systems where data spans structured databases and unstructured legal text.
- The framework supports dynamic prompt engineering tailored to each agent's data source.

### AI4Contracts: LLM & RAG-Powered Encoding of Financial Derivative Contracts
**Authors:** (Anonymous)  
**Venue:** arXiv 2025  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.01063

**Summary:**  
Applies LLMs and RAG to convert natural language financial contract descriptions (term sheets from RBC Capital Markets and JPMorgan) into structured **FINOS Common Domain Model (CDM)** format — the ISDA standard for OTC derivatives. Addresses the data scarcity problem by generating synthetic contract descriptions from existing CDM representations.

**Key Findings:**
- RAG improves coherence and correctness in CDM encoding compared to vanilla LLM prompting.
- Synthetic data generation via LLMs is viable for bridging the natural language ↔ structured contract gap.
- Standardization using CDM reduces reconciliation and supports regulatory reporting.
- A key open challenge: evaluating AI-generated structured outputs for financial contracts lacks established metrics.

---

### LLM Agents in Law: Taxonomy, Applications, and Challenges
**Authors:** (Anonymous)  
**Venue:** arXiv 2026  
**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06216

**Summary:**  
A broad survey covering the transition from standalone legal LLMs to full agentic systems. Proposes a taxonomy of legal agent applications spanning contract review, legal QA, retrieval, compliance, and financial crime prevention. Discusses evaluation methodologies and open challenges for deploying robust legal agents in practice.

**Key Findings:**
- Agentic hybrid RAG frameworks that dynamically route queries between retrieval-based answering and multi-model generation outperform static RAG in legal QA.
- Multi-agent workflows with structured query generation improve retrieval stability across multi-turn legal consultations.
- Financial crime prevention benefits from compliance-by-design agentic architectures with traceability and audit logging.
- Hallucination remains the primary risk; neural-symbolic verification and jurisdictional validity checking are emerging mitigations.

## Research Gaps

Based on the papers above, the following areas remain underexplored and represent potential research opportunities:

- **Consumer loan agreements** as a specific contract type — most existing work targets M&A, derivatives, or fund contracts.
- **Domain-adapted dense retrievers** for financial legal text (current dense retrievers underperform BM25 in legal domains).
- **Evaluation metrics** for structured output generation from financial contracts.
- **Multilingual financial contracts** (e.g., cross-border loan agreements).
- **End-to-end benchmarks** combining retrieval + extraction + summarization for loan contracts specifically.
